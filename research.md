# 401 - Professional Services Automation (PSA)

**Date:** 2026-05-02

---

## 1. Overview

Professional Services Automation (PSA) software unifies the operational backbone of services businesses — project delivery, resource management, time and expense tracking, and client billing — into a single platform. Rather than stitching together separate tools for project management, timesheets, and accounting, a PSA system provides a connected view from project inception through to invoice. The market was valued at approximately USD 16.6 billion in 2026 and is forecast to exceed USD 50 billion by 2034, driven by demand for AI-assisted delivery insights and the elimination of data silos across finance, operations, and client management.

---

## 2. Core Features

- **Project delivery management:** Milestone tracking, task assignment, dependency mapping, and delivery risk alerts driven by AI-based forecasting
- **Resource planning:** Capacity planning, role-based scheduling, skill matching, and utilisation dashboards showing billable versus non-billable time
- **Time and expense tracking:** Mobile and desktop timesheets, receipt capture, mileage logging, and automated policy enforcement
- **Billing and revenue recognition:** Milestone billing, time-and-materials invoicing, retainer management, and compliance with ASC 606 / IFRS 15 revenue recognition standards
- **Analytics and reporting:** Real-time profitability dashboards, pipeline-to-delivery forecasting, and AI-driven scenario modelling
- **Integrations:** CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), ERP (NetSuite, SAP), accounting (QuickBooks, Xero), and HR systems

---

## 3. Market Landscape

The 2026 PSA market spans general-purpose platforms and vertical specialists:

- **Kantata** (formerly Mavenlink + Kimble) — mid-to-large professional services firms, strong resource management and financial reporting
- **Rocketlane** — modern, AI-first platform targeting post-sales delivery teams; emphasises client-facing project portals
- **Ravetree** — broad general-purpose solution favoured for its depth across project management, CRM, and billing in one tool
- **Autotask PSA (Datto)** — purpose-built for managed service providers (MSPs) and IT services firms
- **HaloPSA** — competitive pricing aimed at smaller MSPs and growing IT companies
- **Birdview PSA** — strong fit for creative and design agencies combining project management with creative workflow features
- **Polaris PSA** — cloud-native, targeting technology and consulting firms with AI-driven resource optimisation

Gartner Peer Insights lists this as a mature, competitive market with buyers prioritising AI capability, integration depth, and scalability as top selection criteria.

---

## 4. Key Differentiators

- **AI at the core:** Leading platforms now embed AI for delivery risk prediction, automated handoffs between project stages, and improved time-tracking accuracy (reducing under-capture of billable hours)
- **Client experience layer:** Portals that give clients real-time project visibility without requiring logins to internal tools
- **Revenue recognition automation:** Built-in compliance removes the need for manual journal entries or separate accounting workarounds
- **End-to-end connectivity:** Eliminating the CRM-to-delivery-to-billing gap is the dominant value proposition — deals won in CRM flow directly into project setup and resource allocation
- **Mobile-first capture:** Field-ready time and expense logging that works offline
